#8 - Niki Lauda

The Austrian, Niki Lauda, won 3 Formula 1 world championships. Niki was an incredible driver, but especially in the '70s, and it was

in 1976 when one of the most iconic crashes in F1 history happened. Lauda was way ahead in the championship; it was looking like

Niki was going to take the world championship for the second time. But then Formula One travelled to one of the most dangerous tracks

of all time. The Nürburgring. It had just rained, the track was wet, but also incredibly difficult to drive. And because of F1 safety

back in the '70s, this race was just asking for one more fatality. After Niki's triumph to get the race cancelled, the race still went

on. However, this is when disaster struck. Lauda was zooming round a turn, when he lost control of his car, and was slammed into the

tracks barriers. His car burst into flames. Niki was stuck in the blaze for 55 seconds. Luckily, he managed to escape thanks to the

help of fellow F1 driver Arturo Merzario. The crash nearly killind Niki after he inhaled hot toxic fumes and suffered severe burns.

But instead of giving up, Niki was back racing just 40 days later. Niki carried on racing for 9 more years, when he retired in 1985.


Niki Lauda. A Racing Legend.

By the end of Niki Lauda's tremendous career, he archieved

•3 World Championships

•25 Grand Prix Wins

•54 Grand Prix Podiums

•421 Career Points

•24 Pole Positions

•171 Total Races

season, after his last season with Aston Martin.

#3 - Ayrton Senna

Ayrton. Senna. Brazil's Ayrton Senna will forever be an icon of Formula 1 racing. It was at

Ayrton's 6th F1 race at Monte Carlos, 1984 where the world would see the talent of Senna,

for the very first time. It was raining, very tough driving conditions for even the worlds

greatest drivers. But after qualifying, the grid was lined up with Alain Prost, and Nigel

Mansell taking out the front row. Ayrton started all the way down in 13th, with his

Toleman. The race started, and the rain did not stop. And by lap 23, there had already

been 8 retirements. But soon the rain started to get much worse, so much worse, that now

Prost was waving his hands down the pit straight, trying to call off the race. But by this

point, Ayrton had made it up to second, and still quickly gaining on Alain Prost.

Unfortunately, the race did end up getting cancelled, but Ayrton was happy either way.


Why Ayrton Senna Is One Of The Greatest Of All Time

By the unfortunate end of Ayrton Senna's F1 truely amazing career, he managed to get:

•3 World Championships

•41 Grand Prix Wins

•80 Grand Prix Podiums

•610 Career Points

•65 Pole Positions

•161 Total Races

#1 - Michael Schumacher

Michael Shcumacher. Born in Germany, Michael grew up with a natrual talent for racing. He

started karting when he was just 4 years of age, and was fast; like, really fast. But it

was in 1991, when Michael would debut in the Belgain Grand Prix. That's when everyone saw

Michael's natrual talent. He qualified 7th with his Jordan Ford, pretty good for his

first ever qualifying. Undfortunately, about half way through the opening lap, Muchael's

car had a clutch fail, so he had to retire. However, three years later, Schumacher would

win his first drivers championship, in 1994, the same year Ayrton Senna sadly passed

during the San Marino Grand Prix. Michael also won the championship in 1995, before

moving to Ferrari in 1996. It took a bit for Ferrari to get into tip top shape, but in

2000, everything came together. Michael Schumacher won his 3rd championship in 2000,

before dominating the sport by winning 5 consecutive championships, in 2000, 2001, 2002,

2003, and 2004. Michael is, and will always be an icon of Formula One.
